A Mission to Brighten Young Lives

Providing Free Eye Care for Underserved Children

In India, an estimated 1 in 1000 children aged 0-15 suffer from blindness, totaling 270,000 individuals, the highest number in any single country. Neglecting regular eye checkups, especially prevalent in impoverished settlements, emerges as a significant contributing factor.

Drishti is an initiative started by Sanjana Chauhan, a student from Amity International School, aimed at providing a clear vision and brighter future for Underserved children.

Our Mission

At the heart of our mission is the pressing issue of untreated eye conditions among economically disadvantaged kids.

We believe that no child's potential should be hindered due to poor eyesight. Drishti, with the support of our dedicated volunteer doctors, connects with those in need, offering free eye tests and treatment.

Our Volunteer Doctors

Our team of dedicated volunteer doctors are the backbone of Drishti. They generously offer their expertise and time to provide comprehensive eye care services to children from marginalized communities.

If you're a qualified eye care professional passionate about making a difference, we invite you to join our noble cause. Together, we can brighten the future of countless children.

A Fight Against Eye

Refractive Errors

Including nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism, affecting approximately 2.3 billion people worldwide.

Cataracts

The leading cause of blindness globally, responsible for 51% of blindness cases, particularly prevalent in low-income regions.

Glaucoma

Affects over 76 million people worldwide, with approximately half of cases undiagnosed, leading to irreversible vision loss if untreated.

Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)

Common among children, causing redness, itching, and discharge from the eyes, often due to bacterial or viral infections.
